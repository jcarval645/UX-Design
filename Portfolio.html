GETmobile Redesign

A complete UX design cycle to identify and solve usability issues within the GETmobile app.

Project Overview

This project involved a comprehensive UX design cycle to identify and address usability issues affecting CSULA students who currently use the GETmobile app. Through my research, iterative design, and usability testing, I developed solutions to enhance the app's functionality and improve student satisfaction.

Role

Problem Statement

The Cal State LA GETmobile app suffers from usability challenges that prevent students from being efficient and create negative feelings towards the app. While the app is essential for simple tasks such as viewing class schedules, class information, transportation, and parking details, as well as reviewing financial aid information, current user feedback from surveys and app store reviews indicates that these issues hinder a satisfactory user experience.

"The format of the GETmobile app isn't the most user-friendly. It can be confusing at times, and if it isn't, then sometimes features don't want to load or connect on the app." - CSULA Student

Key Insights

The research showed me consistent issues that impact student efficiency and satisfaction:

  • Navigation Challenges: Students struggle to find specific features, indicating that the format is not as logical as it is.
  • Performance Issues: Reviews mentioned slow load times and a lack of information on the page.
  • User Interface Clutter: The current design is perceived as outdated and cluttered, resulting in content overload that hinders students' quick access to information.

Test Report

As part of a usability testing phase, I conducted a test session with classmates using an interactive prototype. My goal was to assess whether navigation and functionality were efficient.

Test Script

  1. Tap the three icons at the bottom of the screen to ensure you are led to the correct page.
  2. Tap "My Course" and click the report button to ensure it leads you to the "Report A Issue" page and then send! No need to type in name, email, etc.
  3. Tap the grad cap icon at the bottom, and it will send you to the "My Academics" page, including multiple pages. Scroll up and down to ensure it is functional.

Participant Feedback

"I liked the new layout and how interactive it is, being able to click through pages."
"Some icons led to the wrong pages; I am not sure if that is intentional or not."

My Response

After receiving this feedback, I revisited the prototype to review the connection of each page. I noticed that some of the page interactions were leading to the wrong pages; I quickly fixed the issue. For the blank screens, it was likely due to platform loading issues, which are out of my control.

ASI Engagement

I presented the project summary, survey findings, and prototype demonstration to an ASI representative at CSULA via email to gather feedback and ensure the solution aligns with the university's goals and student needs.

Feedback Questions

  1. Do these findings align with the student feedback that ASI receives?
  2. From a leadership perspective, which issue should be addressed first?
  3. Are there some limitations, such as budget or technical, when it comes to improving an app?
  4. After viewing the prototype, what feedback would you give me, design-wise, functionality-wise, etc.?

Response

  1. These findings align with the complaints that the current GET App is slow, hard to navigate, and has a cluttered design.
  2. From a leadership perspective, the issues that should be addressed are moving the apps and icons that are more frequently used depending on students' needs. They should be organized by sections to easily find what the student is looking for, like academics, finances, and campus life, which I like that you have added. There should also be an added page in campus life for advisors, student leaders, RAs if you live on campus and maybe a Dean or a Chair contact for the college that you belong to, to improve the accessibility and awareness of people that are there to assist them.
  3. Limitations that come with updating the app would be a technical issue. I have heard that people would like the GET app and the canvas to be linked, so that as soon as the professor uploads an assignment, it would automatically show up on the home page since the wait time and log in time for canvas is slow. Courses and schedules can be combined into one and more students can have quicker access to viewing their assignments without having to log into canvas every time.
  4. I like that the home page includes less resources, so it isn't overwhelming and cause people to not want to look through everything. I think that having a search bar on the home page so that students don't need to scroll. I like that you created categories for finances, campus life, and academics. I like the overall design, but I feel that the app can benefit from more color integration, so it isn't plain and boring. Using colors aside from gold and black would be a nice change, but not too bright to where it makes it hard to navigate.

Reflection

After contacting an ASI representative, they were able to confirm that the main usability concerns, such as navigation challenges, performance issues, and a cluttered layout, accurately represented the frustrations that students experience, thereby validating my research.

Although this project concludes after the engagement, their feedback allowed me to understand what it is like to receive feedback and guidance from a leadership position. Documenting these insights enables me to close the project and gain a deeper understanding of the importance of understanding users' and stakeholders' perspectives, highlighting the value of collaboration and effective communication in the real world.
